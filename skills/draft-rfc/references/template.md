## Background

What's the current state of the world? What's the issue or opportunity that
makes this RFC worth writing? Concrete numbers, links to incidents, or a
specific user pain are better than abstract statements.

## Proposal

What do you propose, concretely? Be specific about the change, scope, and who
it affects. Avoid hand-wavy verbs like "improve" or "modernize" without saying
what is improving and how.

## Alternatives considered

What else did you consider, and why did you decide against each? Even short
treatments are valuable — they show reviewers you've thought beyond the
obvious path. Drop this section only if there genuinely is only one option.

## Open questions

What's still uncertain? Where do you need input from reviewers before this
can move forward? "None yet" is a valid answer — but only if you really
mean it.
